I started writing this essay after a week of not sleeping well. My roommate keeps the TV on at night and I cannot focus. Anyway, the topic is climate change, which I know something about because my dad works for an energy company and we argue about it at dinner sometimes.

Climate change is real and it is affecting everything. The summers are getting hotter. I remember when I was a kid, we would go to the beach and the water was cold even in July, but now it is warm and kind of gross actually. My mom says the same thing happened when she was young so maybe it is not just me imagining things.

Scientists say the main cause is carbon dioxide and other gases that come from burning fossil fuels. I looked this up for another class and it seems pretty straightforward. The problem is that everyone knows this but nobody wants to change. Oil companies spend millions on ads that make it seem like there is still a debate. There is not a debate. The science is clear.

What can individuals do? Not that much honestly. You can recycle and take shorter showers but that is a small thing compared to what corporations do. I think the real change has to come from laws and regulations. Countries need to stop subsidizing coal and oil. That money could go to solar and wind energy which are getting cheaper every year anyway.

I do not have a great conclusion here. It is a hard problem. But I think the main thing is that young people need to vote and care about this because our generation will actually live with the consequences, unlike politicians who will be dead.
